Grandma's Apple Pie

Prep: 30 mins
Serves: 8

Ingredients

  • 2½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 cup cold butter, cubed
  • ¼ cup ice water
  • 6 cups thinly sliced Granny Smith apples
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 2 tbsp flour
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp nutmeg
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 egg, beaten (for wash)
2

Instructions

  1. Mix flour, salt, and 2 tbsp sugar. Cut in butter until crumbly. Add water 1 tbsp at a time until dough forms.
  2. Divide dough in half, flatten into disks. Wrap and chill for 1 hour.
  3. Preheat oven to 375°F. Roll one disk to fit a 9-inch pie plate.
  4. Mix apples, sugar, flour, spices, and lemon juice. Pour into crust.
  5. Roll second disk, cover pie. Crimp edges, cut slits for venting.
  6. Brush with beaten egg, sprinkle with sugar.
  7. Bake 45-50 minutes until golden and filling bubbles.
  8. Cool completely before serving.
Grandma's secret: Add a pinch of cardamom and a splash of brandy to the filling for extra depth of flavor!

Hearty Tomato Basil Soup

Prep: 15 mins
Serves: 6

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 2 28oz cans San Marzano tomatoes
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves, torn
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste
4

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onions, cook until translucent (about 5 minutes).
  2. Add garlic, carrots, and celery. Cook until vegetables begin to soften, about 8 minutes.
  3. Add tomatoes, breaking them up with a wooden spoon. Add broth, tomato paste, and oregano.
  4. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes.
  5. Add half the basil. Using an immersion blender, blend until smooth (or transfer carefully to a blender in batches).
  6. Return to heat, stir in cream, and warm through. Do not boil.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve topped with remaining fresh basil.
Perfect with grilled cheese sandwiches! I like to use crusty sourdough bread and sharp cheddar.

Double Chocolate Fudge Cake

Prep: 25 mins
Serves: 12

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • ¾ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 2 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • ½ cup vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup hot coffee
  • 8 oz semisweet chocolate chips
Celebration Family Favorite
6

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, cocoa, baking soda, baking powder, and salt.
  3. Add buttermilk, oil, eggs, and vanilla. Beat with mixer on medium speed for 2 minutes.
  4. Stir in hot coffee (batter will be thin). Fold in chocolate chips.
  5. Pour batter evenly into prepared pans.
  6. Bake 30-35 minutes until toothpick inserted in center comes out clean.
  7. Cool in pans for 10 minutes, then remove to wire racks to cool completely.
  8. Frost with your favorite chocolate frosting between layers and over top and sides.
This was Johnny's birthday cake every year from age 7 to 18! The coffee really brings out the chocolate flavor, but you can't taste the coffee itself.

Lemon & Herb Roasted Chicken

Prep: 20 mins
Serves: 4-6

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken (4-5 pounds)
  • 2 lemons
  • 1 head of garlic, halved crosswise
  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • 3 tbsp fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
  • 2 tbsp fresh sage, chopped
  • 1 tbsp kosher salt
  • 2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup chicken broth
Sunday Dinner Comfort Food
8

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F.
  2. Pat chicken dry with paper towels. Place in a roasting pan.
  3. Zest one lemon into a small bowl. Quarter both lemons.
  4. Mix lemon zest, herbs, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  5. Gently loosen skin from chicken breast and thighs. Rub half the herb mixture under the skin.
  6. Rub softened butter over the outside of chicken, then coat with remaining herb mixture.
  7. Stuff cavity with quartered lemons and garlic halves.
  8. Tie legs together with kitchen twine and tuck wing tips under body.
  9. Pour broth into pan. Roast for 1 hour and 15 minutes, or until thermometer inserted into thickest part of thigh reads 165°F.
  10. Let rest 15 minutes before carving.
Dad always says this reminds him of Sunday dinners at Grandma's house. The drippings make amazing gravy!
